About

Mediaeval Field Cemeteries in Central Bohemia (MFC-CB) is dedicated to collecting, harmonizing, and publishing data on inhumation cemeteries in Central Bohemia from the 9th to the 11th century through the THANADOS interface.

Before the mid-9th century, the population of Central Bohemia practised a specific, likely cremation burial rite, which left no archaeological traces. The emergence of field cemeteries signifies a profound social transformation within the population of Central Bohemia during the formation of the early Premyslid state. The decline and gradual abandonment of these burial sites are intimately linked to another significant social process — the Christianization of Bohemian societies during the 10th century, primarily in the first half of the 11th century.

The project

MFC-CB is a thematically focused project that provides accessible data through the THANADOS interface. It specifically concentrates on one of the most prominent subjects in early medieval Archaeology in Central Europe, which is the study of flat (inhumation) cemeteries that appeared in various regions from late antiquity to the beginning of the high medieval transformation. All information regarding the presented sites is derived from original publications or proprietary datasets, and it is presented as a digital catalogue featuring interactive maps and visualizations of the cemeteries. The project primarily focuses on the region of Central Bohemia. If you are interested in collaborating on data production or publishing your own dataset pertaining to other regions of the Czech Republic, please contact the project team (contact details provided below).

The MFC-CB initiative has emerged from the long-term research activities conducted at the Department of Historical Archaeology and the Department of Informational Sources and Landscape Archaeology of the Institute of Archaeology of the CAS in Prague. The acquisition of data is supported by the funding program Strategy AV21 of the Czech Academy of Sciences.

License and data re-use

The data is published in accordance with an open data access policy and with the consent of copyright holders. Data usage is subject (unless explicitly stated otherwise) to a CC-BY 4.0 (Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International). The data can therefore only be used with citation of the author/provider. This provision does not restrict the use of MFC-CB by virtue of other contractual arrangements or by virtue of a legal title.

As a component of the Archaeological Information System of the Czech Republic (AIS CR) infrastructure, we predominantly provide data in an open mode (Open Access). However, we do not have the capability to monitor the usage of this data or track the resulting outputs. Nevertheless, we have a legal obligation to report this information, so we kindly request your cooperation as a data user to report any scientific outputs generated using the data or other components of the AIS CR.

Each user is therefore obliged to:

- always indicate in the usual manner in specific outputs that the AIS CR infrastructure has been used to produce them, referring to the data or tool used;
- when entering output information into Results Information Index - RIV (e.g. at Czech Academy of Sciences sites via the ASEP database), select the proper link of the result to the AIS CR infrastructure in the field of LRI (Large Research Infrastructure) from the offered project code list of large research infrastructures;
- kindly let us know via this questionnaire (https://www.aiscr.cz/vystupy), that the output has been published.
